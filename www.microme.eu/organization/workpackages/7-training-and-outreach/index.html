7 - Training and outreach

Workpackage No.: 
7
Workpackage Title: 
Training and outreach
Activity Type: 
DEM
Objectives: 
Raise awareness of the value of Microme throughout the scientific community; harness community expertise in the curation of Microme
Description: 
Outreach activities to a wider community of research in Systems Biology, Microbiology and Bacterial genomics are essential for the project, as the success of Microme will ultimately depend on its community of users and curators. Task 1 Organisation of Annotation Jamborees A series of annotation jamborees will be organized in collaboration with the European Science Foundation Programme “Frontiers of Functional Genomics” that has as one of its goals the organization of this type of activity. These jamborees will provide intensive training for curators, will raise awareness of Microme in the scientific community, and will contribute to the population of the resource. Task 2 Organisation of Microme Training Courses Microme will also provide a series of training courses targeted both at end-users (microbiologists and biochemists interested in Microme mostly as a reference ; and computational biologists /systems biologists interested in using Microme pathways, models and computational tools for their own analyses) and actual or potential curators (genome annotators interested in curating a specific genome using the Microme infrastructure, including microbiologists responsible for bacterial sequencing projects at CEA and possibly WTSI; and domain experts potentially interested in curating their pathways of interest across many species using the Microme infrastructure). In practice, both categories can be accommodated in the same training session by extending a general introductory course with hands-on training in metabolism-related bioinformatics with specific sessions for curators and bioinformaticians. Eligible participants will be not only graduate students, but also junior or senior researchers who stand to benefit from the use of Microme, and expert curators who can add significant value to the Microme datasets by completing or correcting pathways (WP2) and models (WP3). These training courses will be organized in collaboration with established training venues. Many of the beneficiaries have extensive experience of organizing such courses and maintain large lists of contacts throughout the bioinformatics and microbial communities who will be contacted by email and invited to apply to take part. Of particular importance are the preexisting networks of microbiologists and biochemists that are already involved as curators or collaborators with the two sequencing centers (CEA and WTSI), or as curators with the SwissProt/UniPathways project (SIB). Would-be curators for a specific set of species can be targeted using these lists. Participants will be invited to send a short scientific resume with their application. Since these courses are meant to train expert curators to use the Microme infrastructure, participants will be selected on the basis of their competence in microbiology or biochemistry. In the event of a course being oversubscribed, participants will be selected by the organizing beneficiaries according to the relevance of their scientific experience, and with a view to maintaining a balance between industrial and academic beneficiaries. Priority will be given to applicants who have been unsuccessful in a previous application. Following the model established by the Biosapiens and ENFIN NOEs, the courses will take place once per year, every year in a different country. Five staff of different groups will be responsible for training students in expert curation and bacterial metabolic analysis using the project tools and methods. The demonstrated advantages of this model include providing personal relations between the staff, creating a teaching material that remains from course to course and disseminate the tools and methods in the community of users. In addition, the main European, US and Asian conferences where the communities targeted by Microme (microbiologists, systems biologists, biochemists, bioinformaticians) congregate will be used as hot-spots for the organization of community activities, including the organization of specialized meetings as satellite workshops and the user meetings as ad-hoc conference activities. The specific choice and detailed planning of dissemination activities for each year will be performed by the Management Committee.
